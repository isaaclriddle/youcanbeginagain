**Early Beginnings**

*You Can Begin Again* was born out of the experiences of a little boy who didn't know he was a boy sitting down to play Obsidian Entertainment's *Fallout: New Vegas* for the first time. As what the trans community colloquially calls a ‘baby trans,’ my cobbled-together performances of masculinity were kept sequestered to the world of video games as I created my very first Courier Six: ‘Atlas.’ I was addicted from the first time an in-game character referred to me as male. It wasn’t before long that Atlas shrugged the world off his shoulders and morphed into ‘Cain’ in *Fallout 3* and *Far Cry 5,* then ‘Cato’ for a few campaigns of *Dungeons and Dragons,* and finally ‘Isaac’ when I inevitably returned to the Mojave Wasteland to begin again. In many ways, that makes this work auto-ethnographic; it is a collection of other trans people’s experiences as much as it is directly reflective of my own.

This archive, like the people whose stories it contains, is also both a product of and a response to its surroundings. Created from the intermixing of a beta archive hosted on Scalar and a critical analysis paper discussing Obsidian Entertainment’s *Fallout: New Vegas,* it is a project that should be understood as something iterative. The version that exists now is a response to limitations related to time, labor, and financial support being externally enforced upon it, which is to say that it must adhere to cissexist methods of constructing knowledge environments in order to come into being. In that sense, it also takes inspiration from Dr. K.J Rawson’s [Digital Transgender Archive](https://www.digitaltransgenderarchive.net/) in that this project acknowledges the constraints forced upon it and has resolved to prioritize the “increased open access to trans history” in order to “complicate(s) normative histories” of gender-diverse and trans experiences in video game spaces.[^1]

Future iterations of this archive aspire to draw elements of the embodied presentation of knowledge present in Danielle Braithwaite Shirley’s [Black Trans Archive](https://blacktransarchive.com/), which forces users to reckon with the humanity of its archival objects through the usage of an avatar creation system.[^2] *You Can Begin Again* is much earlier in its transition. It has taken the plunge – changing its name, buying new clothes, acquiring its first prescription –  but it is still in the early stages of becoming something new. It is intimately aware of the limitations that it faces and the cis scrutiny it will inevitably encounter. Still, it does its best to stand as proudly as it can, donned in shades of pink, white, and blue. Please forgive the awkward cracks in its voice; it is still trying to find it.
